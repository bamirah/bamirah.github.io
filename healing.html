What is healing?

Parameswaran et al. (2023) define healing as “a multi-layered, ecological process that addresses wellbeing and resilience on the individual, school, and community/societal level” (p. 187). Healing is a process of reconnection- reconnective to ourselves (body, mind, and spirit), our communities, our cultures, and the land surrounding us. We can approach healing work through somatics. Why focus on healing-centered engagement when trauma-informed care exists?

A healing-centered approach requires us to shift our focus from trauma, its causes, and its manifestations to helping our students become agents of change (Ginwright, 2018). This is why I consider trauma to be what you know and healing to be what you do. You can’t do the work of healing without knowing what you hope to heal from. Gobir (2022) breaks healing-centered engagement down into 5 components:

Agency: develop student voice and encourage collective engagement
Culture: have restorative conversations about identity
Relationships: create empathetic and vulnerable spaces
Meaning: understand your purpose
Aspirations: goal setting
