“For these are all our children, we will all profit by or pay for what they become.” - James Baldwin

I welcome you to this website with a quote by my favorite writer and civil rights activist, James Baldwin. We owe so much to our children, especially the promise that schools value their full humanness. In a place where we require their daily attendance, they have every right to feel joy, safety, care, and most importantly - love. Without the commitment to these rights, they will and should grow up to resent and resist us. We must orient our work towards prioritizing trauma and healing if we are serious about socially just schools. The world is burning. We either do what we can to put out the little fires everywhere, or become consumed by them until we’re nothing but ashes.

Thank you to Dr. Sharim Hannegan-Martinez for believing a better world is possible and doing “all the things” to build it, including teaching Trauma Practices and Healing-Centered Pedagogies, a community I am fortunate to have been part of and the space that inspired me to create this resource. Thank you to the scholars who have contributed to the knowledge surrounding trauma and healing in schools, in addition to other bodies of research. Thank you to the educators who will engage in this critical work. Thank you to Lawrence Bitzer for using his computer engineering skills to help me design and build this website. Thank you to anyone who deeply cares.

With Love,
Amirah B
